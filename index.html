Tour de France 2023 Route

The 2023 Tour de France Grand Depart will be a big one as it takes place in cycling heartland, the Basque Country on the Spanish side of the border. This is an area with a rich cycling tradition and super passionate supporters. The race kicks off on July 1 and finishes on July 23. As is tradition, the Tour de France will finish in Paris.

Discover Le Tour de France 2023 Route

First Stages

The first stages will take place on the Basque Country, on the spanish side border. This is an area with a rich cycling tradition and super passionate supporters. Then the Tour will pass to the french side to countinue the race for the next 3 weeks.

Mountain Stages

The tour will have its classical route through the Pyrenees and the Alps. It will also pass through the Central Massif. Key stages will be the following: Stage 6 (Pyrenees) with the ascention of Col de Tourmalet. Stages 13, 15 and 17 (Alps) with ascentions to Grand Colombier and Col de la Loze.

Favourites

Two times champion Tadej Pogacar will be the man to beat. Despite finishing second last year, he has returned stronger, more matured and motivated and in the start of 2023 he is proving everyone he is stronger than ever. Jonas Vingegaard , last year's champion, will try to defend his title. A really dangerous climber and really complete rider with an extremelly strong team. Other candidates are: french young rider David Gaudu, Simon Yates, Jai Hindley and colombian Daniel Felipe Martinez (in case he starts as the leader).

Possible record breaker

Mark Cavendish, the veteran sprinter, will try to break Eddy Merckx record of most stages wins by a single rider. Now riding for team Astana, he will try to beat younger sprinters to get that final historical victory.
